My mom’s hobby is making origami with money; she spends hours researching different templates, folding, composing, mounting and framing her work. My dad works at a bank and whenever she starts a new project she’ll pester him to exchange her worn out twenties for a freshly minted stack of ones. Her signature are owls(her favorite animal) though she's made everything from clothing, animals, geometric compositions and even themed works for Father's day and Valentine's day.

A few years ago she created her own her own template for the Chinese god of wealth, Caishen, and instructed that I keep it in my wallet so that he could bring me wealth and fortune. She keeps a few spare ones on her and gives them to friends and family though on occasion she’ll give one to a friendly stranger to which, as is asian tradition, they find a way to promptly return the favor. We’ve been given dessert when she gave one to the owner of a local local Chinese restaurant we frequent and on another occasion free cashews from a salesman from Japan.
